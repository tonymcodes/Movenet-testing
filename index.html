How it works: MoveNet returns 17 body keypoints per frame via TensorFlow.js. For the chosen exercise this prototype picks 3 keypoints (e.g. hip → knee → ankle for squats), computes the joint angle with atan2, then a two-state machine (UP ⇄ DOWN) counts a rep each time the angle crosses both thresholds. Keypoints below a confidence score are ignored to reduce jitter. Everything runs on-device — no video leaves your browser.
